Earth’s particular physico-chemical conditions have allowed the apparition of life, but also its evolution into more complex forms. Through time, ecosystems have developed in such a way that the very core of the energy forming process begins with photosynthesis, which derives its energy from the sun to create organic compounds such as glucose. That said, earth's abyss has shown that there are other ways to generate such energy, to make an ecosystem flourish. Considering this, it is conceivable that life could exist in the deep oceans of exoplanets, driven by abiotic factors that create conditions suitable for life. In such environments, fauna would rely on chemosynthesis to generate organic materials and energy. But what might one of these hypothetical worlds look like?
